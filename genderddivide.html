Gender Based Digital Divide

Internet Penetration of Active Users in India in 2021




Ownership of Mobile Phones in India in 2021

As you can see, there is a huge gap between the amount of men using the
internet and the amount of women using the internet. There is also a big gap
between men and women living in urban and rural areas. Internet penetration
of men living in urban and rural areas is 71% and 54% respectively while that
of women living in urban and rural areas is 57% and 34% respectively.In the
year 2021, 49% of men owned mobile phones compared to only 26% of women.
This gendered digital divide is often a result of a triple disadvantage for women
in India.

First, there is a rural-urban digital divide, where rural internet penetration is
only 29% compared to the national average of 51%.

Second, there is an income-based digital divide between households preventing
women from these households from owning digital devices.

Finally, discrimination within the household prevents women from equitably
accessing digital devices within the domestic sphere, which in turn widens the
gender-based digital divide.
