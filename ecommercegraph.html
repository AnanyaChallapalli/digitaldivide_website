Digital Divide in Ecommerce

Only 10% of Indian MSMEs (Miro Small and Medium Enterprises) have embraced
e-commerce. For those who have, they have experienced increased sales, profitability,
and market penetration. However, for the remaining 90%, the rise of digital commerce
is not an opportunity, but a threat. On one hand, MSMEs that have integrated into
digital platforms are reaping significant benefits. A striking 35.5% of e-commerce
integrated MSMEs reported a turnover between Rs 10 million and Rs 500 million
in the 2021-22 financial year. Contrastingly, only 15.6% of non-integrated MSMEs
achieved this scale of turnover.

The lack of technology adoption can limit the ability of MSMEs to innovate, optimise
their operations, and scale their business. Sometimes, MSMEs often face challenges
in accessing technology due to the high cost of investment, lack of awareness, and
limited technical expertise.
